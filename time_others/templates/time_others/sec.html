Week 1 (today)

After all participants have correctly solved the minimum requirement of transcription tasks, the task of today's laboratory session is to make a series of allocation decisions for different situations. In each decision, you have to decide about the allocation of a certain number of transcription tasks. There are nine different situations in total. They differ in terms of who is affected by the allocation of the tasks and the information available to you. Some decisions affect only you and other decisions affect only another person.

Once the decisions are over, one of these situations will be randomly selected (for more on this see below). This choice determines how many tasks you and the other person have to solve today and next week. Only one decision will be chosen, but it is in your best interest to decide in each decision as if it were the one that counts. The different situations are classified into nine blocks. The blocks are as follows:

  • Block1: In block 1, you have to decide how many tasks you want to complete today and how many tasks you want to complete next week.
  • Block 2: In block 2, you have to decide how many tasks you want your randomly.
    • matched partner to complete today and how many tasks you want her to complete next week.
  • Block 3: In block 3, you will repeat the decisions from block 1, but with one twist. You will also receive information about how many tasks your matched partner has been randomly assigned to complete between today and next week (if the decision is selected to be the allocation that counts), and then you will decide your allocation as in block 1. Note: The allocation of tasks that your partner would need to complete will only be valid if that decision is selected to be the allocation that counts. There is no accumulation of tasks across decisions.
  • Block 4: Block 4 is analogous to block 3, but the roles are reversed. In block 4, you will receive information about how many tasks you have been randomly assigned to complete between today and next week (if the decision is selected to be the allocation that counts), and then you will decide the allocation for your matched partner as in block 2.
  • Blocks 5 and 6: Blocks 5 and 6 are similar to blocks 3 and 4, respectively, but the difference is that the total number of tasks to be allocated is different and the random assignment may change as well.
  • Block 7: In block 7, you have to decide simultaneously how many tasks you want to complete today and next week, and how many tasks you want your partner to complete today and next week. You have a separate budget of tasks for each allocation decision. In other words, each decision in this block actually involves two, which resemble the ones from blocks 1 and 2. However, both are linked to each other, in the sense that if a simultaneous decision is selected to be the one that counts, both you and your partner have to complete their respective allocations, as you decided.
  • Block 8: In block 8, you have to make decisions similar to the ones in block 1 or 2. Whether the decisions are similar to block 1 or 2 is randomly assigned.
  • Block 9: In block 9, you have to allocate tasks between you and your partner. You have to decide how many tasks you want to complete today and how many tasks you want your partner to complete today as well, subject to one joint budget.

En cada decisión de esta tarea, tanto usted como su compañero tienen fortunas opuestas. Es decir, lo que obtendría en el Estado A es igual a lo que recibiría su pareja en el Estado B. De manera similar, lo que obtendría en el Estado B es lo que obtendría su pareja en el Estado A. Dado que usted y su pareja enfrentan destinos opuestos, su cuadrado azul y el círculo naranja de su compañero siempre están en posiciones especulares en el gráfico. En la captura de pantalla, por ejemplo, su cuadrado está en (Estado A = 50, B = 16,7) y el círculo de su compañero en (Estado A = 16,7, B = 50). Esto significa que, si ocurre el Estado A, usted recibe 50 y su compañero obtiene 16,7; y, si ocurre el Estado B, usted obtiene 16,7 y su compañero obtiene 50. Al comienzo de cada ronda de decisión, el cuadrado y el círculo aparecerán en una posición aleatoria. Para elegir, arrastre el cuadrado o el círculo a las ubicaciones elegidas. La otra forma se ajustará automáticamente a la posición del espejo.

Para esta tarea, decide sobre su propio sorteo. Consulte la figura a continuación para ver un ejemplo. Aquí los estados A y B ocurren cada uno con un 50% de probabilidad. Para esta tarea, la fortuna de su pareja es fija y debe elegir su propio sorteo. Dado que usted y su pareja enfrentan diferentes fortunas, su cuadrado azul y el círculo naranja de su pareja estarán en diferentes posiciones. En la captura de pantalla, por ejemplo, su cuadrado está en (Estado A = 35, B = 9) y el círculo de su compañero en (Estado A = 10, B = 20). Esto significa que, si sucede el Estado A, usted recibe 35 y su compañero obtiene 10; y si ocurre el estado B, obtienes 9 y su compañero obtiene 20. Puedes mover tu cuadrado pero no puedes mover el círculo de tu compañero. Además, tenga en cuenta que las probabilidades de cada estado pueden variar en diferentes rondas de decisión. Al comienzo de cada ronda de decisión, el cuadrado aparecerá en una posición aleatoria. Para hacer una elección, arrastre el cuadrado a su ubicación de preferencia.
